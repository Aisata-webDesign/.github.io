Flurries

A poem by Aisata Ba

I wonder how snow feels when it comes flurrying down.

From the heaventop it floats, gracing the dry mound that is earth.

Once lush and bountiful, now a barren wastland from the harsh harsh winter.

Empty it is--dead leaves and cold hearts

Void of feeling, numbingly cold.

Like frigid icicles breaking and shattering onto hard pavement.

I wonder how snow feels when it comes flurrying down.

When it comes drifting in the chilling wind with a mystical dance.

A white blanket of curtseying snowflakes on what once was.

A reminder that there is a frigid beauty even in the coldest of hearts.
